Season 1



In Season 1, we're introduced to the brilliant yet eccentric detective Sherlock Holmes, played by Benedict Cumberbatch, and his loyal friend Dr. John Watson, portrayed by Martin Freeman. Together, they tackle a series of intriguing cases in modern-day London. From solving the mysterious deaths in "A Study in Pink" to unraveling the secrets behind "The Great Game," this season establishes the dynamic duo's unique crime-solving partnership.



A Study in Pink: Sherlock and Watson meet for the first time as they investigate a series of mysterious deaths where victims are found with pink-painted pills. They uncover a deadly game orchestrated by a cabbie.

The Blind Banker: Holmes and Watson delve into the world of Chinese smuggling and ancient symbols as they investigate a murder connected to a bank.

The Great Game: The dynamic duo faces Moriarty's challenges, solving puzzles to prevent bombings across London. The episode builds to a thrilling climax on the rooftop of St. Bart's Hospital.

Season 2



Season 2 delves deeper into the complex characters of Sherlock and Watson. The season kicks off with the unforgettable "A Scandal in Belgravia," featuring Irene Adler, the enigmatic dominatrix. The suspense escalates with "The Hounds of Baskerville," a gripping adaptation of Sir Arthur Conan Doyle's classic tale. Finally, "The Reichenbach Fall" takes us on a rollercoaster ride of emotions as Holmes faces his arch-nemesis, Moriarty, in a thrilling showdown.



A Scandal in Belgravia: Sherlock encounters Irene Adler, a dominatrix with compromising photos of a royal figure. He must outsmart her and protect the monarchy's secrets.

The Hounds of Baskerville: The duo investigates the death of a man in the eerie Dartmoor, where rumors of a supernatural hound abound. They uncover a sinister experiment.

The Reichenbach Fall: Moriarty orchestrates a series of high-profile crimes, putting Sherlock in a moral dilemma. The episode culminates in a showdown at a rooftop swimming pool.

Season 3



Season 3 explores the aftermath of Sherlock's apparent death in the previous season. Sherlock returns to London, much to the surprise of John, in "The Empty Hearse." The season continues with "The Sign of Three," where Sherlock must navigate the complexities of being John's best man. "His Last Vow" sees the return of a dangerous adversary, Charles Augustus Magnussen, challenging Holmes' intellect and morality.



The Empty Hearse: Sherlock returns from his apparent death and reunites with Watson, who has moved on. Together, they solve an underground terrorist threat.

The Sign of Three: : Sherlock faces a new challenge as he takes on the role of best man at Watson's wedding. He must juggle wedding responsibilities with solving a murder mystery.

His Last Vow: Holmes confronts the ruthless media mogul Charles Augustus Magnussen, who holds powerful secrets. The episode explores Sherlock's addiction and moral choices.

Season 4



The fourth season delves into the darker aspects of Sherlock's life and relationships. "The Six Thatchers" begins with the mysterious destruction of Margaret Thatcher busts, leading to a web of conspiracy. "The Lying Detective" sees the arrival of a new nemesis, Culverton Smith, while "The Final Problem" takes Holmes and Watson to a remote island where they confront a long-buried family secret and a chilling villain.



The Six Thatchers: Sherlock investigates the mysterious destruction of Margaret Thatcher busts, leading to a complex web of espionage and international intrigue.

The Lying Detective: :Holmes takes on the case of a wealthy philanthropist who seems to have a dark secret. He also confronts his own addiction issues.

The Final Problem: Sherlock and Watson are lured to a remote island, where they face a series of deadly puzzles set by Holmes' estranged sister, Eurus. The episode reveals hidden family secrets and challenges Sherlock's intellect and emotions.
